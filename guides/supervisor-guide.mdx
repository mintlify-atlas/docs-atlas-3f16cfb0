## Your Role in the System

As a supervisor, you are the **operational coordinator** of the workshop. Your job is to:

✅ Distribute work among operators  
✅ Monitor what's happening on the plant floor in real-time  
✅ Detect and resolve delays before they reach the client  
✅ Register workshop resources and materials

## Main Screen: The Dashboard

When you log in, you see the **Management Dashboard** with metrics for the selected period.

### Main KPIs

<Accordion title="Production KPIs">
  - **Open Orders**: Total active work orders in the system
  - **In Process Now**: Work orders with operators working at this moment
  - **Delayed**: Work orders past their delivery date without closing ⚠️
  - **Closed**: Work orders completed in the period (3M / 6M / 12M)
  - **Avg Time/WO**: Average hours per order
  - **Active Operators**: How many are working this shift
</Accordion>

### Period Filter

Use the **3M / 6M / 12M** buttons to view data from the last quarter, semester, or year.

### Charts

- **Production Trend**: Closed work orders + average hours per month
- **Order Status**: Donut chart with distribution by status
- **Revenue vs Cost**: Estimated billing vs real material cost
- **Monthly Margin**: Gain/loss month by month

### Alerts

<Tip>
If there are work orders with time or cost deviations, they appear in the **"Alertas operativas"** (Operational Alerts) section with two levels:

- 🟡 **WARNING**: Near the limit
- 🔴 **CRITICAL**: Exceeded threshold, needs immediate intervention
</Tip>

## Dispatch Center (Supervisor)

This is **the most important screen for daily work**.

Find it in the menu → **"Despacho"** (Dispatch).

### What Does It Show?

**Shift KPIs (top row):**
- Work orders in process / Paused / Delayed / Active operators

**Work Orders in Execution:**

Each card shows the active work order with:
- Code + title + client
- Status (color: green=in process, orange=paused, red=delayed)
- Assigned operators and resources
- Delivery date

**Operators on Shift:**

List of who is working on which work order

**Pending Queue:**

Next 10 work orders to dispatch, ordered by priority

**Delayed Table:**

Overdue work orders with days of delay

<Note>
This screen automatically updates every 30 seconds.
</Note>

## Work Order Management

From **"Órdenes"** (Orders) in the menu:

### Creating a Work Order

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Creation Form">
    Click **"Nueva OT"** (New Work Order)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill Details">
    Complete: title, client, description, delivery date, priority, estimated hours
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link Quotation (Optional)">
    You can link it to an approved quotation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create">
    Click **"Crear"** (Create)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Assigning Operators and Resources

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Work Order">
    Open the work order detail
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign Resources">
    In the **"Recursos asignados"** (Assigned Resources) section, select the operator or machine
  </Step>
  <Step title="Operator Sees Work Order">
    The operator will see the work order in their "Mi Turno" (My Shift) screen
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Changing Work Order Status

Valid statuses and their flow:

```
PENDIENTE → PLANIFICADA → EN_PROCESO ⇄ PAUSADA → FINALIZADA → ENTREGADA
```

You can change status from the orders list using the status selector or from the detail view.

### Useful Filters

<Accordion title="Filter Options">
  - By **status**: view only IN_PROCESS, PAUSED, etc.
  - By **client**: search work orders for a specific client
  - By **operator**: view workload for a person
</Accordion>

## Quotation Management

From **"Presupuestos"** (Quotations):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Quotation">
    Indicate client, items, and estimated values
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client Approves">
    When the client approves, convert it to a Work Order
  </Step>
  <Step title="Convert to Work Order">
    Click **"Convertir a OT"** — the work order is created with quotation data
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Workshop Resources

From **"Recursos"** (Resources) you can view and manage:

- **Human Resources**: operator name in the resource system (different from user)
- **Machines and Tools**: lathes, milling machines, equipment, etc.

Resources are assigned to work orders to register which person or machine does each job.

## Materials

From **"Materiales"** (Materials) you can:

- View the materials catalog with unit price
- Add new materials
- View historical registered consumption
